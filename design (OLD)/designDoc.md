# Mobile UI
- I want to go for a portrait style game for the mobile version because I feel like with landscape, the buttons to move would get in the way of what the world has to offer
  - To make sure this problem doesn’t happen, I gained inspiration from a gameboy style system where only half of the screen is the actual gameplay while the other half is the buttons you use to play the game.
    - I decided on this system because it didn’t make sense to use a joystick since the game was limited by the movement based on the 2D array (We can only move up, down, left, and right) and I decided on the format of a gameboy because having a d-pad by itself on the screen didn’t seem to look pleasant/ it felt out of place and since a d-pad can’t really be dynamic as a joystick, I traded off more space for the map for a button set up that looks better
    - We were also considering having a swiping mechanic, but we figured that the continouous swiping would be too tedious for the user. We tried improving on this idea with swiping and holding to replace continuous swiping, but we don't know how hard that would be to implement.
    - I also want the major buttons to be comfortable for the user on mobile to use without any major strain on their fingers by understanding how the thumbs are going to be oriented
  - I also have some of the useful but not really used buttons on the top for the player’s convenience of not having a crowded bottom half and accidentally going into a setting they didn’t really want to
  - The photo I have is only of the main world, so the UI for the profile, the settings, the inventory, and the combat haven’t been made yet, but some of these do require more communication, specifically with the group making the map and the group deciding the combat.
- **UPDATE**: After actually making the blueprint for the UI, we decided to make some major changes
  - We moved the inventory button and the settings button to the top left and top right respectively and removed the Map button and the character button
    -We moved the inventory button and settings button because with the removal of the map and character button, the top part felt empty. We also moved the inventory and settings button because there could be times where there may be accidental misclicks with the palms (from my experience with typing, i accidentally change the language in my phone sometimes). Not only that, we decided to place the inventory and settings button to an area where one would actually expect to see a settings and inventory button.
    - We removed the map and character button because when we made the first draft, we forgot to account for the front camera, which may obstruct the view. We also believe that A) The map button may be unnecessary for mobile/in general because of the top down view (we can maybe have a general shape of the world, but we feel like that may be too niche for it to be a major button) and B) The character customization feature may be accessed by clicking the player name, but there is also a chance that we don’t have character customization in the first place.
  - Because we moved the inventory and settings button, there is unoccupied space, so we were also thinking about having our logo in either the bottom left or bottom right corner of the Mobile UI
